Daniela Reséndiz
Bio CV Publications Projects Blog ES

Specialist in urban policy, spatial planning, and climate adaptation with over eight years of experience advancing social, territorial, and environmental justice. Bridges public policy, applied research, and technological tools (GeoAI) to turn evidence into high-impact decisions.

She has led multidisciplinary teams and collaborated with governments, international organizations, and academia on disaster risk reduction and climate change policies across housing, mobility, and urban development. As Director of Risk Management in the Government of Mexico, she advanced official standards, prevention and reconstruction programs, and specialized technical projects.

She holds a MSc in spatial planning (space syntax) from The Bartlett, UCL (London), with additional training at the Architectural Association and the University of São Paulo, and a Bachelor’s in Architecture from UNAM (with honors). Skilled in programming, spatial statistics, network analysis, remote sensing, and climate simulations.

Alongside professional practice, she teaches at UNAM and the School of Public Administration, and has represented Mexico at international forums —including the UN— engaging with countries such as Japan, the Netherlands, Kenya, and Italy.
